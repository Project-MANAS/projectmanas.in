  • Don’t cry over Fallen Presents (easy)

    • It’s Christmas time and Project MANAS is getting ready to open their presents. Unfortunately, Shivansh is a bit clumsy and falls over the presents by mistake. The presents are actually gift hampers so their contents get spread on the floor by mistake. Thankfully, the damage is not much and one can understand which item belongs to which hamper. In addition to being clumsy, Shivansh is very lazy so he wants you to do that. You are given the floor dimensions and the number of presents and your goal is to identify the centre of the fallen items of each present. The fallen items are represented as 1s and the empty spots as 0s. Output the centers.
    Note: Make sure to handle the input in the same order as given. (consider the bottom left as the origin)

  • Don’t cry over fallen presents(Hard)

    • (Extension of the easy part) Everything was going fine for Shivansh but then Anshumaan came into the room and saw the fallen presents. As a physics lover, he wanted to understand the different patterns and boundaries of the fallen items for his upcoming paper on entropy. He wants you to give information about the boundaries of each fallen gift. Do this by returning the coordinates of the points that are located exactly on the boundary.
    Note: Please ensure that the enclosing polygon is convex. The points on the said polygon need to be coordinates of the boundary items for that hamper.


  • Need for Speed : Eve Wanted (Easy)

    • It’s time for Project MANAS to upgrade their PC parts but unfortunately they cannot be shipped to us. AI members Ishika and Aryan decide that they would drive down to the seller and collect it themselves. Since both of them do not have a driving licence it’s up to EVE, our self driving car to take them to their destined location. Your mission, should you choose to accept it, would be to write a vector based navigation solution that avoids all obstacles on their way to the store. Due to the extremely short battery life of EVE and the fact that Ishika was too lazy to fully charge it, ensure that navigation output is the shortest possible path. EVE uses a PID controller to navigate which relies on a heading angle and position to correct the path. Therefore, the objective is to output the position and heading angle of EVE at points along your generated path at intervals of a fixed magnitude.

    Note that you will be penalised for vectors that enter obstacle zones as well as for discontinuous paths.


  • Need for Speed: Eve Wanted (Hard)

    • There's a small problem: most shops are closed today because of a festival. There is one PC shop in Manipal that is open, but it's located in an area by the beach with sandy soil, rocky terrain, and a lot of puddles. Time for EVE, our self-driving car, to go off-road! In this question, each obstacle has a predefined traversability value (or 'cost') between 1 and 10. For example, shallow puddles can quite easily be passed (they have low cost = 1), sand is more difficult but still traversable, while rocks are impossible to pass through (with the highest cost = 10). Since EVE has sensitive electronic components, driving over difficult obstacles can cause a lot of damage to our club's hard work (and yearly budget). Avoiding all obstacles which are impossible to pass through (with cost = 10), find an optimum path that causes least damage to EVE. Don't forget that EVE also has low charge, so a very long path is not a great idea!

    Please note that we are using a custom checker for scoring. Multiple answers can generate the same score. Multiple submissions are allowed for all challenges.

  • Hardware and PCB Designing Challenge

    • Since Christmas is near, Darshan wants his house to be decorated with twinkle lights. But right before he can place an order on Amazon, his brother, Vishal, gets hold of him and puts forth a challenge. If he can build him a circuit that can flash at least two LEDs, he’ll buy as many lights as Darshan wishes. Of course, the real gift is for Darshan to understand and appreciate how the beautiful patterns that he loves are brought to life.

    Easy

    The task is to design a simple voltage regulator.

    Using an LM317 voltage regulator IC, design a PCB capable of supplying voltages between 1.25-27 V at 1.5A. Use the datasheet for reference. Check below for specifications.
    A wheel of mass 4Kg and radius 2m is rotating about x axis with an angular momentum of 5kgm2/s. If now it is rotated about the Z-axis by an angle of 30° in 2 sec, calculate the direction and magnitude of the new angular momentum. Assume thickness of wheel is negligible.
    Include:

    • Appropriate Resistors for minimum power loss.

    • Appropriate connectors on the Input and Output sides.

    • Suitable protection features.

    Design a single layer PCB along with schematic using Autodesk Eagle.

    Hard

    The task is to understand and implement the 555 timer IC.

    With the help of a 555 timer IC, design a circuit drawing power from the voltage regulator in the previous task that flashes two generic LEDs alternatively such that Ton = Toff, this frequency should be adjustable.
    A wheel of mass 4Kg and radius 2m is rotating about x axis with an angular momentum of 5kgm2/s. If now it is rotated about the Z-axis by an angle of 30° in 2 sec, calculate the direction and magnitude of the new angular momentum. Assume thickness of wheel is negligible.

    Changing frequency

    Use the datasheet for reference. Include appropriate current limiting resistors and integrate both onto the same PCB.

    Specs Value
    Supply voltage - LM317 36V battery
    Supply Voltage - 555 10V
    Maximum dimensions of PCB without 555 timer 3x3 cm
    Maximum dimensions of PCB with 555 timer 6x6 cm
    Judging Criteria

    • The efficiency of the Circuit and PCB

    • Appropriate Component Selection (price and availability)

    • Size of PCB

    Bonus Points

    Reverse protection on the Input side, Proper grounding techniques, Simulation of the Circuits.

    The designs can be made on KiCAD or EAGLE. Submissions have to be in the form of a zip file containing a circuit diagram, schematic, and board layout, and bill of materials (excel sheet). 2nd Year Applicants have to attempt both the tasks.


  • Embedded Systems Challenge

    • Nilabha is a naughty kid. He wants to prank his teammates by changing the circuitry of the windscreen wiper of Eve (Manas’s Beloved Autonomous Car). Help him realize his imagination by building the system he wants.

    Parts Description
    Assume you are given an Arduino Uno, 4 pushbuttons, 2 servos, and a potentiometer.

    Each of the Four Buttons has specific Functions

    • 2 Pushbuttons which will be referred to as BUTTON1 and BUTTON2 here on

    • 1 Pushbutton for changing the different Modes of execution (Explained Further Ahead) and would be called MODE

    • 1 Pushbutton to Reset the whole program which will be called RESET

    Modes

    There will be Three Modes that can be changed using the MODE pushbutton.
    The Mode Descriptions are :

    • On the Press of BUTTON1, both the servos must increase their angle, and on the press of BUTTON2, they must decrease their angle.

    • On the Press of BUTTON1, servo 1 must increment its angle whereas servo 2 must mirror the first servo. On the Press of BUTTON2 servo, 1 must decrement its angle, and servo 2 must mirror.

    • In Mode 3 the Servos should Sweep from angle 0° to 180° gradually and then from 180° to 0° (Just like a Windscreen Wiper) where the Potentiometer changes the speed of the Sweep.

    After taking any such sequence as input, the pressing of the ALL_OFF button should cause the LEDs to glow in the same sequence, with the speed of change to the next state being decided by the input from the potentiometer, similar to Pulse mode.

    The answer to the Embedded Challenge has to be submitted as a link to the Simulation.

    Hints: Use of arrays may help design the above system. The use of Arduino is preferred for this question. Simulating is easy on Tinkercad . Name all variables in the code appropriately for more readability and easy debugging.

  • Planning and Control System Challenges

    • Tushar and Dhruva have built a 4-wheel differential-drive robot (Skid-steer drive specifically). They made it a hobby project but are now occupied with research work on FPGAs. It is now up to you to configure the robot and complete the field tests. The robot contains an IR temperature sensor on the bottom of its body, pointing to the ground and giving you the floor’s temperature below it. An inbuilt positioning system gives you Odometry, including position and angular orientation(x, y, z, θ) at any point in time.

    LEVEL-1 TASK

    The first task is to find the hottest and coldest points in the field (both unique) and then travel from origin to the hottest point, followed by the coldest point followed by the point whose temperature is equal to the average of hottest and coldest temperatures (take the closest smaller integer if the values don’t match). Every time you reach one of these points, the robot stops and deploys a sensor to monitor temperature for Tushar continuously. Design an approach and write pseudocode to complete this task and explain how you can interpret the grid system from the real world.

    Assumptions:

    • Assume that you receive the position of the target from the command center in the form of (x, y) coordinates w.r.t the bottom left of the bounded field which is to be considered the origin I.e., (0, 0) in meters every timestep.

    • Also assume that you know your global position (x,y,theta) in the field (w.r.t the origin) from an in-built positioning system.

    • Robot always starts from origin(0,0,0).

    • Max speed of the robot: 5 m/s

    Expected Output: You must output the control commands i.e., speeds of the left and right wheels of the bot in m/s every timestep.

    LEVEL-2 TASK

    After finishing the first task, there were heavy rains, and our positioning system failed to work due to the bad weather, and the deployed sensors need to be collected as they are malfunctioning. You have reached the origin after the first task and now need to travel by treating the sensors as obstacles. Choose appropriate sensors before starting out to be able to detect the obstacles.

    You can add a maximum of 3 more sensors of a maximum of 2 different types of your choice to customize your robot further. Design an approach and write pseudocode to complete this task and provide supporting arguments for the choice of sensors.

    Modify the approach in order to make this system work satisfactorily. The assumption that robot always starts from origin still holds.

    Expected Output: Same as Level 1.

    Expected answers: A simple pseudo-code in any language will suffice. Along with this, you will need to provide the velocities of each pair of wheels throughout the process with equations. You can also provide some text describing what you are trying to do in the pseudo-code.

    Judging Criteria

    • Efficiency of the algorithm or approach used.

    • Total cost of the added sensors.

    • Methods used for controlling the traversal.


    Hints: You could look into control theory for moving the bot around.

    The mechanical challenge is a bonus round for you to skip the whole interview phase and directly get in the task phase. The challenge is divided into two parts, Mechanics and Design challenge. As a second year it is compulsory to attempt both. The challenge is meant to test your innovative skill as well as your mathematical foundation. The deadline is 9th December.

  • Mechanics

    • Easy

    Find the angle Θ when the figure is in equilibrium. OA= L,AB = 2L, BC = L/2. ∠OAB = 90. ∠ABC = 60

    Medium

    You are playing a video game and to complete the level you must cross a rotating disc with angular speed = 5 rad/sec and radius = 10m. The character spawned at Point A at the center of the disc and you must reach point B in a straight radial path. The character can move with a constant speed Sm.

    • What should be the minimum speed (Smin) so you can traverse the disk in a straight line from Point A to B wrt ground.
    • Find the time needed to traverse from A to B,radial distance as a function of time,radial velocity as a function of time & radial acceleration as a function of time for speed Smin.
    • What will happen if his walking speed is less than Smin. and why?

    Hard

    An elastic string of natural length √2 L m has one end, A, fixed and the other, B, attached to one end of a uniform rod BC of length L m and mass M. This can turn freely in a vertical plane about its other end C, which is fixed at a distance L m vertically below A. Initially the rod is vertical, and, on being slightly displaced, falls until it is horizontal (extension in the string is small), and then rises again to AC. Find the elastic constant (k) of string.
